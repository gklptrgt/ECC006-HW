a) Why was the Internet Society created?

In 1992, the Internet Society (ISOC) was formed by a group of individuals who played a crucial role in inventing the internet, such as Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn. Their primary goal was to ensure that the internet was used to benefit people globally.

The ISOC's key responsibility is to guarantee that the internet is open and available to everyone, enabling innovation, economic progress, and social advancement. The organization brings together a diverse range of individuals, including technology experts, policymakers, and businesses, who are passionate about the internet.
